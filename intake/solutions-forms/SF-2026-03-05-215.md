# Solution Request #215 - LTTS
**Date:** 2026-03-05  |  **Status:** Solution Given Closed  |  **Complexity:** Low Complexity

## Subject
LTTS_Feasibility check for enhancements

## Brief
LTTS has requested for 3 enhancements for their nomination process. Kindly review them and let us know how it can be catered.

## Problem Description
Basket Nomination: Nominators are uploading multiple nominations within a single tab. This makes it cumbersome to approve or decline individual nominations, as there is no option available to act on each nomination separately within the tab.
 
Duplicate Nominations: The system allows multiple nominations for the same individual, which makes it difficult to review and decline all duplicate entries individually through the portal.
 
Nomination Criteria:
Currently, peer-to-peer and self-nominations are enabled in the portal, providing nomination access across all grades. This defeats the intent of the recognition program, as it is monetary in nature.
Hence, it is important to restrict nomination eligibility to IS or individuals with direct reportees. We have observed several peer-to-peer nominations for reasons such as “good team player”, which can be adequately recognized through non-monetary appreciation programs.
Additionally, peer nominations require validation with the IS, which further makes the process cumbersome and time-consuming. Limiting the nomination criteria will help maintain the integrity of the program and streamline the approval process.

## Solution Given
Please find below our understanding and solution for each query:
1. Basket Nomination
Current Understanding:
The client currently has awards that are being used for both individual and team nominations. Due to this setup, if a nomination includes multiple individuals and the approver wishes to reject only one nominee, they are forced to reject the entire nomination.
Proposed Solution:
Our platform allows flexibility in configuring award types. The client should be advised to create separate award categories for Individual and Team nominations. Clear policy guidelines should also be established to ensure:
Individual awards are used only for single-employee recognition.
Team awards are used exclusively for group achievements.
2. Duplicate Nomination
Current Understanding:
An individual can currently be nominated multiple times, which may lead to duplicate nominations for the same employee within a short timeframe.
Proposed Solution:
Certain validations and governance rules can be implemented by the Tech Ops team to restrict duplicate nominations. For example, a rule can be configured to prevent an individual from being nominated more than once within a month.
These rules must first be defined by the client, after which our team will review and confirm technical feasibility.
Please note that duplicate nominations cannot be restricted for Panel Awards.
3. Nomination Criteria
Current Understanding:
The client would like to maintain exclusivity for certain awards by restricting self-nominations and limiting nomination rights to specific employee grade groups.
Proposed Solution:
This requirement can be managed using the Advanced Filters option available on the dashboard. The filters can be configured based on the attributes available in the Employee Details Table (e.g., grade level, role, department) to control nomination eligibility and permissions.
