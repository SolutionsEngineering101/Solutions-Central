# Solution Request #210 - NDCP
**Date:** 2026-02-26  |  **Status:** Solution Given Closed  |  **Complexity:** High Complexity

## Subject
National DCP - Contract cancellation

## Brief
NDPC Contract Cancellation

## Problem Description
1.	Username/password reset issues
Majority (almost 80%) of their workforce is blue collared such as truck drivers. These users find it incredibly hard to remember their username/password. To add salt to the injury, NDCP HR had also sent them reset password link for UAT environment, which resulted in them not receiving password reset emails. Our UAT environment emails don’t to external recipients. 

2.	More than expected time needed at their end to administer the program
The constant need to reset passwords, see who has been able to successfully redeem or not, get catalog enabled again has resulted in more than excepted time for the HR folks to administer the program.

3.	Service yearbook has PII details about city/deptt
Service Yearbook (share your thoughts link) has PII listed such as department/city/state.

4.	Email reminders not inline with the reminder day/period
Email reminders haven’t gone out as per frequency. Meant to go out on 3 & 7 days but haven’t been going out as per expectations. Someone times 1 of them is missed, sometimes both or something totally erroneous.
Guess, in a one-off instance, user had access to multiple year catalogs.

5.	Different invoices - 5yrs vs 25 yrs
Their catalog is configured via amazon milestone for 5/10/15/20.
For years -25/30/35/40 etc, its configured via E2E. 
This results in different reports for them and the invoicing frequency isn’t timely too.

## Solution Given
National DCP’s concerns around login challenges, administrative effort, PII compliance, reminder emails, and dual catalog complexity have been reviewed.

A dual login option (Email + Password and Email-based OTP) can be enabled to address frequent password reset issues. Email IDs can also be updated on an ad hoc basis since Employee ID is the primary key, ensuring no impact on historical data.

PII fields (city, department, state) in the Service Yearbook can be removed and have been validated in UAT. Reminder emails are being triggered as configured; specific cases can be reviewed if shared.

To resolve catalog and invoicing fragmentation, a consolidated milestone model using Snappy is proposed. Confirmation from the Snappy team is currently awaited before proceeding further.
