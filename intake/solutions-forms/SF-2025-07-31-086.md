## Solution Given
Following a detailed discussion with Subhendu and also took suggestions from Anjan. The consensus is clear: rather than going ahead with the multi-wallet approach (Option #2), we recommend shifting to a direct allocation model as an approach to avoid significant operational overhead across multiple teams.
The secondary wallet model introduces several risks:
Scheduler dependencies for point movements
High chances of human/manual misses in case of bulk redemptions
Increased pressure on support, CX, and engineering for reconciliations and exception handling
Complications in scaling or automating this flow cleanly
Revised Approach: Direct Allocation Model
Instead of crediting 100% of the reward value, we allocate only 60% directly to the user’s Primary Wallet at the time of award.
The remaining 40% is not passed to Vantage and is to be treated by Inovalon internally as the tax component (withheld or grossed-up via payroll).
Users will see only the Primary Wallet and have immediate access to the full gift card catalog — no secondary wallet, no restrictions.
Vantage Circle will only bill Inovalon for the 60% value that is actually fulfilled on-platform.
This model offers a much cleaner and scalable structure:
Simplifies platform logic and operations
Ensures real-time reward visibility for users
Keeps tax control fully with Inovalon’s payroll team
Eliminates the need for redemption data pulls, post-processing, or wallet-to-wallet transfers
I've rewritten the entire documentation reflecting this approach, including benefits, examples, and impact.
Notes on Option 1 & Option 2
Both Option 1 and Option 2 would require custom technical development on our end.
If Inovalon chooses to proceed with either of these two models:
A detailed Business Requirements Document (BRD) will need to be created
The estimated development effort is approximately 40 man-days for option 2 including testing.
While for option 1 it will be lower but we should still consider 5-10 man-days including testing.
