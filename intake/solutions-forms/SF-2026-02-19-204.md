# Solution Request #204 - Celebal Technologies
**Date:** 2026-02-19  |  **Status:** To Product Closed  |  **Complexity:** Not Set

## Subject
New Requirement from Celebal Technologies

## Brief
The client has requested that the nominator’s name be included in the subject line of the auto-triggered email sent to the recipient. Although we clarified that the nominator’s name is already mentioned within the body of the email, the client emphasized that visibility in the subject line significantly influences open rates. According to them, employees typically scan emails within seconds, and seeing the nominator’s name upfront would create a sense of importance and urgency, prompting immediate attention and action rather than deferring it. The ultimate goal is to increase engagement.

## Problem Description
The client has requested that the nominator’s name be included in the subject line of the auto-triggered email sent to the recipient. Although we clarified that the nominator’s name is already mentioned within the body of the email, the client emphasized that visibility in the subject line significantly influences open rates. According to them, employees typically scan emails within seconds, and seeing the nominator’s name upfront would create a sense of importance and urgency, prompting immediate attention and action rather than deferring it. The ultimate goal is to increase engagement.

## Solution Given
Adding the nominator’s name to the award email subject line is unlikely to improve engagement, as data shows low platform usage is driven more by lack of structured triggers than by email copy. Engagement spikes only when there is an active push, and Teams logins are significantly underutilized. The recommended actions are to activate structured campaigns and review the Microsoft Teams adaptive card setup rather than modifying subject lines.
