# Solution Request #19 - Bata
**Date:** 2025-05-01  |  **Status:** Solution Given Closed  |  **Complexity:** Medium Complexity

## Subject
Support Required on Award Configuration Feasibility – Bata

## Brief
1. Achievers League – Quarterly - Individual/Team Award

Client Request: Approval flow to go to the nominees’ respective HODs (for both individual and team awards), with budget allocation from the nominee's department.

Challenge from VC: Approval routing to each nominee's HOD is not feasible if team nominations involve cross-functional or cross-departmental members.

Proposed Alternative from VC:
Achievers League (Individual) – Approval to nominee’s HOD
Achievers League – Team – Approval to nominator’s HOD

Client Response: The client is not in agreement with the two-award workaround and insists on a unified award structure with approval to the nominee's HOD, regardless of individual or team award.

2. CEO Champions – Annual Award

Client Request: Multi-level approval involving HOD → CHRO → CEO.

Nominee eligibility is - Any employee who has received cross-functional 4 Monetary On-Spot Awards and a minimum of 1 Quarterly Award

Challenge from VC: This nominee's eligibility is not currently supported in the platform’s architecture.

Proposed Alternative from VC: Manual Points Upload via the dashboard using a CSV file. Extract of eligible users can be calculated based on a report from existing nomination and appreciation data.

Client Response: The client is not in agreement with the workaround and has expressed concerns that both features were indicated as feasible during the sales stage, leading to a sense of disconnect.

## Solution Given
Achievers League – Quarterly - Individual/Team Award
The award configuration will remain unchanged, with the approval flow continuing to route to the respective HODs of the nominees, whether for individual or team awards. However, nominators will be required to select only one approver in the case of Team Awards. This approach is intended to prevent system conflicts, as sending approval requests to multiple approvers could lead to inconsistencies if even one approver rejects the nomination. Therefore, it is recommended that approval requests be routed to a single approver only.

The budget for the awards can be deducted from a centrally allocated budget.

CEO Champions – Annual Award
Before proposing a workaround for this, we want more information:
We understand the nomination eligibility, but who will have the nomination rights? ANS- RM
Is this a one-winner or multiple-winners award? ANS - Multiple Winners
Would it be multiple nominations for one winner or a case-by-case award? ANS - Case-by-case
Will it be multi-level, going from regional to national? ANS - 

Eligibility criteria based on prior award history are not currently available. Since this is an annual award, eligible employees may be identified through the Nomination Report or Manager Insights. Subsequently, nominators may proceed with the standard nomination process, which entails three levels of approval for each nominee.
