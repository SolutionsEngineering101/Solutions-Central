## Solution Given
Process Details: How exactly do we "facilitate" the process when a client uses their own vendor? Can you provide a step-by-step breakdown of how this would work? If a client has a preferred vendor, we support them through the following process (available only in India):
The client's vendor is onboarded on mutually agreed business terms.
The client's vendor shares the SKUs of their merchandise items requested by the client.
We add these items to our merchandise catalog on the platform.
Employees can place orders through this catalog as they normally would.
We then coordinate order fulfillment offline directly with the client’s vendor.
If the client does not have a vendor, we also have in-house vendor options available to support their needs.

We discussed the "public recognition" opt out feature, but it sounded like more research needed to be done to confirm the options.  

Please provide a screen shot or an example of the options that employees have for opting out of different types of programs (birthdays, anniversaries, Greetings, other recognition/awards).  Do they have to opt out of everything, or can it be itemized?
 
Employee have two preferences they can manage from their profile:

- Show my birthday on the social feed
- Require my consent and approval before any recognition(s) are published on the platform feed

By deselecting the birthday preference, employees can completely opt out of birthday posts.
When employees select the approval preference, the system will wait for their consent before publishing any recognition posts publicly. If the employee declines approval, the post will not be published.

If they opt out, then please confirm they still receive a private notification for the "appreciations" and "nominations."
Opting out simply means the system will wait for the recipient's approval before publishing the recognition post to the platform feed. Recipients will still receive email notifications for any appreciation or nomination, along with a platform notification prompting them to approve or decline the public post.
