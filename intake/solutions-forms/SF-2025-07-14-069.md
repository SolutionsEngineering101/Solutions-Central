# Solution Request #69 - Bosch
**Date:** 2025-07-14  |  **Status:** Open  |  **Complexity:** High Complexity

## Subject
Requirement from Bosch- Critical

## Brief
Bosch has 8 entities in India—4 are live on Vantage Rewards, and 4 (approx. 1000 users) will onboard soon.

Employees often contribute across entities, but current separate instances prevent cross-entity recognition. We need a solution to enable this.

## Problem Description
Bosch operates through 8 different entities in India, out of which 4 (top 4) are currently using Vantage Rewards. The plan is to onboard the remaining 4 entities (approximately 1000 users) shortly.
Employees often contribute to tasks and initiatives across entities. For example, someone from Entity A may support a project or task for Entity B.
The HR or Manager from the benefiting entity (Entity B) would like to recognize and reward such cross-functional contributions. However, since each entity currently operates its own instance/tool, this is not technically possible today.

Current Limitation
      •     Recognition is confined within each entity’s environment.
      •     There is no cross-entity recognition or points transfer mechanism.

Potential Expectations
      •     Enable managers/HRs to reward users from outside their entity.
      •     Possibly a unified backend that allows inter-entity visibility and secure cross-entity recognition.
      •     Ensure auditability, compliance, and data segregation as required by Bosch.

Strategic Benefits for Vantage Circle
1. Accelerated Expansion Across Bosch
      •     Bosch has already adopted Vantage Rewards in 4 out of 8 entities.
      •     This use case becomes the driving factor to onboard the remaining four entities faster.
      •     Positions Vantage Circle as a scalable, enterprise-grade solution, solving not just transactional needs but structural pain points.

2. Platform Consolidation = Higher Retention
      •     Unifying recognition across entities improves adoption and dependency.
      •     Reduces the likelihood of Bosch considering other vendors for the remaining entities.
      •     Locks in a larger share of the wallet across the Bosch ecosystem.

3. Higher Engagement = Higher Redemption = Higher Revenue
      •     Cross-entity recognition leads to more recognition transactions.
      •     More points distributed → More points redeemed → Increased redemption revenue for VC.

4. Stronger Business Case for Other Multi-Entity Clients
      •     Bosch can serve as a marquee reference for complex, multi-entity recognition implementations.
      •     Opens doors for similar enterprises with federated HR structures (e.g., Tata, Renault Nissan, Aditya Birla, etc.)

5. Deeper Platform Stickiness
      •     Solving this workflow binds VC deeper into Bosch’s HR processes.
      •     Makes it more challenging to move away from the platform once cross-entity dependencies are in place.

6. Opportunity for Premium Billing/Upsell
      •     The technical lift and additional capabilities can be positioned as a premium add-on or custom module, justifying upsell discussions.
