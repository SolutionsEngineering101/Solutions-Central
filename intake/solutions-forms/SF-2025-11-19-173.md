# Solution Request #173 - Sunpharma
**Date:** 2025-11-19  |  **Status:** Solution Given Closed  |  **Complexity:** Low Complexity

## Subject
Acknowledgment: Sun Pharma Spotlight Award Distinction Request

## Brief
Sun Pharma will have two Spotlight awards—one auto-approved and one with an approval workflow. Since both share the same name, they want a clear visual distinction on the frontend (e.g., a small label or marker on the award image).

Could you please confirm if this is feasible?

## Problem Description
Sun Pharma would like to have two awards with the same name—Spotlight—one auto-approved and the other requiring an approval workflow. Since both awards will appear with the same name, they want to ensure users can clearly distinguish between the two from the frontend.

They are asking if we can visually differentiate them (for example, a small label or marker on the award image indicating “Auto-Approved” vs. “Requires Approval”) so nominators can easily identify which one to select.

Could you please confirm if this is feasible?

## Solution Given
Could you please help us understand the use case in detail behind this, in terms of the following, or any additional information you may have:
Dual Workflows:
Why does the same award have two workflows - one that is auto-approved and another that requires approval levels? Is there a difference in award amounts or other criteria that determines when each workflow should apply?
Nominators for Auto-Approved Version:
Who are the intended nominators for the auto-approved version of the award?
Award Visibility:
Are all nominators expected to see both versions of the award, or should visibility differ based on role or eligibility?
Use of Advanced Filters:
Have we explored using advanced filters to ensure that only eligible nominators can view and submit the auto-approved version?

Any technical customization would require substantial effort, including feasibility assessment, documentation, development, and QA.

Therefore, I am outlining a few non-technical alternatives. These may already have been considered by the team, but understanding the client’s perspective beforehand will help us determine whether technical exploration is necessary.
Nomenclature of the award:
We understand the client’s preference to retain the existing award name. However, given that the award values differ, it may be advisable to introduce a slight variation in the naming—such as Spotlight Silver and Spotlight Gold—to clearly signify the respective tiers.
Visual identifier:
If the client prefers to keep the award name unchanged, the badge design can still visually differentiate the tiers. For instance, a silver-toned badge for the lower-value award and a gold-toned badge for the higher-value award. This creates a logical and intuitive distinction, ensuring both peers and recipients can recognize the relative value of each award.
Award description:
If neither of the above options aligns with the client’s preferences, the brief award description visible in the nomination window can be used to help nominators distinguish between the award levels. However, in our experience, visual differentiation is the most effective approach in scenarios like this.
